Introduction

Let's discuss what would happen if magnetic monopoles existed!

It's impossible to create these monopoles by cutting a magnet in half, even if we break it down to its individual atoms and electrons:

Image credit: Wikipedia user Sbyrnes321.


However, let's imagine they do exist and make the following observations (with N for "north pole" and S for "south pole"):

  • N pushes N away,
  • S pushes S away, and
  • N and S are pulled toward each other.

We can summarize it by saying, "Like poles repel, and opposite poles attract."


Also, the magnetic field lines, labeled B, would look like this (compared to electric field lines, labeled E, for electric charges):

Image credit: Wikipedia user Maschen.

We also notice that a moving magnetic charge creates a circulating electric field (similar to how a moving electric charge creates a circulating magnetic field).

Formulas

Field formulas

The gravitational field and electric field formulas are

  • Fg = mg
    (force equals mass times gravity)
  • Fe = qE
    (force equals electric charge times electric field strength)

Similarly, there must be a magnetic field formula for magnetic monopoles, with qm representing magnetic charge:

Fm = qmB
(force equals magnetic charge times magnetic field strength)


The SI unit for magnetic charge can be found by dividing both sides by the magnetic field strength B:

Fm/B = qm

The unit for force is a newton (N), and the unit for magnetic field strength is a tesla (T). A tesla can also be written as a newton per ampere-meter (N/A·m).


Units for qm:

  • N/T
  • = N / (N/A·m)
  • = N * (A·m)/N
  • = A·m

Therefore, the SI unit for magnetic charge is the ampere-meter (A·m).


Force formulas

Now let's think about the force on one magnetic monopole due to another. The gravitational force and electric force formulas are

  • Fg = G * m1m2/r2    (Newton's law of universal gravitation)
    (G = 6.674 * 10-11 N·m2/kg2)
  • Fe = k * q1q2/r2    (Coulomb's Law)
    (k = 8.99 * 109 N·m2/C2, where C stands for "coulomb")

It follows, then, that the magnetic force formula is

Fm = km * qm1qm2/r2
(Coulomb's Law for magnetism)

where qm1 and qm2 are magnetic charges and km is the magnetic constant (similar to the electric constant k).


Local field formulas

Similarly, there are formulas for the local gravitational/electric field:

  • g = GM/r2   (local gravitational field)
  • E = kQ/r2   (local electric field)

Thus, the formula for the local magnetic field is

B = kmQm/r2
